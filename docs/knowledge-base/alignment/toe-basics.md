# Toe basics (car)

Toe is the angle of each wheel relative to the centerline of the car, viewed from above. Toe-in points the fronts of the tires toward each other. Toe-out points them away.

## Front toe

- Front toe-out (1-3 mm total): sharper turn-in, more responsive feel, slightly less stability on straights, slightly more tire scrub.
- Front toe-in: more stability, lazier turn-in, less tire scrub.

Most track cars run a small amount of toe-out for turn-in response.

## Rear toe

- Rear toe-in (1-3 mm total per side): stable under throttle and braking, reduces oversteer. Most street-based track cars run rear toe-in.
- Rear toe-out: rotates the car aggressively, can be unpredictable under throttle transitions. Rare on track cars.

## Common symptoms

- Lazy turn-in with front toe at zero: try 1-2 mm total toe-out.
- Nervous straight-line tracking with large toe-out: reduce by 1 mm and re-evaluate.
- Snap oversteer on throttle: check rear toe; loss of rear toe-in from worn bushings is a common culprit.

## Safe increments

Adjust toe in 0.5-1.0 mm increments. Measure at ride height, not jacked up. Always record the before/after values so you can revert.
