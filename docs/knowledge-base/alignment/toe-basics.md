# Toe basics (car)

Toe is the angle of each wheel relative to the centerline of the car, viewed from above. Toe-in points the fronts of the tires toward each other. Toe-out points them away.

All values below are **per side** unless explicitly marked "total". A "total" figure is split evenly between left and right (e.g., 2 mm total toe-out = 1 mm per side). Always measure at ride height with the driver's weight (or ballast) in the seat; jacked-up readings are misleading.

## Front toe

- Front toe-out (0.5-1.5 mm per side, i.e. 1-3 mm total): sharper turn-in, more responsive feel, slightly less stability on straights, slightly more tire scrub.
- Front toe-in: more stability, lazier turn-in, less tire scrub.

Most track cars run a small amount of toe-out for turn-in response.

## Rear toe

- Rear toe-in (0.5-1.5 mm per side, i.e. 1-3 mm total): stable under throttle and braking, reduces oversteer. Most street-based track cars run rear toe-in.
- Rear toe-out: rotates the car aggressively, can be unpredictable under throttle transitions. Rare on track cars.

## Common symptoms

- Lazy turn-in with front toe at zero: try 0.5-1.0 mm per side of toe-out.
- Nervous straight-line tracking with large toe-out: reduce by 0.5 mm per side and re-evaluate.
- Snap oversteer on throttle: check rear toe; loss of rear toe-in from worn bushings is a common culprit.

## Safe increments

Adjust toe in 0.25-0.5 mm per-side increments (about 0.5-1.0 mm total). Measure at ride height with driver weight in the seat, not jacked up. Always record the before/after values so you can revert.
