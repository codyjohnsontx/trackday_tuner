# Rebound vs compression

It is easy to confuse the two. Use this checklist before adjusting.

## Compression clues

Symptoms show up while the suspension is being compressed:

- Hard impact feel over sharp bumps.
- Nose-diving excessively under braking.
- Loss of grip on turn-in, with the front tire chattering or skipping.
- Rear squatting so much on throttle that the front unloads.

If the symptom is at the moment of input (bump, brake, throttle), suspect compression first.

## Rebound clues

Symptoms show up after the input, as the suspension extends:

- Suspension feels like it hops or pogos after a bump.
- Chassis wallows through long, flowing corners.
- Packing down on repeated bumps (suspension cannot recover between hits).
- Corner-exit rear grip fades over repeated laps.

If the symptom persists after the input, suspect rebound first.

## A common trap

Too much rebound feels similar to not enough compression because both leave the suspension sitting lower than ideal. Check chassis attitude: if the car/bike is riding low and harsh, stiff rebound is likely. If it is diving into bumps, soft compression is likely.

## Adjusting safely

Make one click at a time on one axle at a time. Run at least half a session before judging. Revert quickly if feel gets worse; do not stack multiple changes hoping to cancel them out.
