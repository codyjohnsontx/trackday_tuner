# Motorcycle sag

Sag is the difference between fully extended suspension and the loaded position. Measuring sag is the starting point for any suspension setup on a motorcycle.

## How to measure

Use the L0/L1/L2 method:

- L0: fully extended suspension (rear wheel off the ground, front unloaded).
- L1: static sag - bike on the ground, no rider, wheels loaded.
- L2: rider sag - rider in full gear, feet up, bike balanced.

Static sag = L0 - L1. Rider sag = L0 - L2.

Measure the front at the fork tube or axle-to-triple-clamp reference. Measure the rear from the rear axle to a fixed point above it (taillight bracket or subframe). Always measure from the same reference.

## Interpreting the numbers

- Rider sag at target, static sag at target: spring rate and preload both correct for your weight.
- Rider sag at target, static sag too low: spring is too soft; preload is masking it. Consider a stiffer spring.
- Rider sag at target, static sag too high: spring is too stiff. Consider a softer spring.
- Rider sag too high, static sag at target: add preload (within range).
- Rider sag too low, static sag at target: remove preload (within range).

## Track vs street

Track setups typically run less sag than street setups for quicker response and a geometry that loads the front on entry. Do not copy a track-spec sag onto a street bike without understanding the tradeoffs in ride quality.
