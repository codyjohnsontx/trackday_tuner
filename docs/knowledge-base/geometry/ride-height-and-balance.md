# Ride height and balance

Small geometry changes have large effects on steering feel. Make one change at a time and measure.

## Fork height in the triple clamps

Sliding the forks up in the triple clamps (more tube showing above the top clamp) lowers the front of the bike. This reduces rake and trail, quickens steering, reduces high-speed stability. Sliding them down raises the front, increases rake and trail, slows steering, adds stability.

Typical increments: 2-3 mm at a time. Anything more is a big change.

## Rear ride height

Raising rear ride height (longer shock link or more preload, depending on configuration) steepens rake, quickens steering, and shifts weight forward. Lowering rear ride height slackens rake, slows steering, shifts weight rearward.

## Combined effects

- Quicker steering: raise rear or drop front.
- More stability: drop rear or raise front.
- Too much of either direction destabilizes the bike. Work in small steps.

## Symptoms and likely causes

- Bike feels lazy on turn-in but stable at speed: geometry is biased too stable. Try dropping the front 2 mm or raising the rear.
- Bike is nervous on straights and has a wandery front: geometry is too quick. Try raising the front 2 mm or lowering the rear.
- Front pushes mid-corner only, stable everywhere else: this is usually a tire or damping issue, not geometry. Rule those out first.
