# Aero basics (car)

Aero downforce grows with speed squared. Changes that feel small in the paddock become large at the end of a straight. Make changes in small increments and always consider the speed range they affect.

## Rear wing angle

More wing angle = more rear downforce and more drag. Use rear wing to cure high-speed rear instability or to bias the balance rearward. Too much wing costs terminal speed on straights.

## Splitter

A front splitter generates low-pressure air under the front and increases front downforce. Extending or lowering the splitter adds front downforce, which sharpens high-speed turn-in but can cause oversteer if the rear is not matched.

## Rake

Rake is the difference between rear and front ride height (rear higher than front is positive rake). Positive rake increases the effective angle of attack of the floor and adds rear downforce. Reducing rake shifts balance forward.

## Balancing

Aero balance must be coherent with mechanical balance. A front-biased aero setup on a mechanically understeering car can over-rotate at high speed. When you change one aero element, expect to revisit the others.

## Symptoms at speed

- Stable through slow corners, loose at speed: rear aero too low. Add wing angle or rake.
- Understeer that gets worse with speed: front aero low for the rear. Lower splitter or reduce rear wing.
- Drag-limited on straights: too much wing; reduce 1-2 clicks and re-evaluate lap time, not top speed alone.
