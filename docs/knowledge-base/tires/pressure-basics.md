# Tire pressure basics

Tire pressure controls contact patch shape, carcass stiffness, and operating temperature. Small changes (0.5-1.0 psi) can meaningfully change grip and feel.

## Cold vs hot pressures

Cold pressure is measured before the tire has worked. Hot pressure is measured immediately after leaving the track, when the tire is at operating temperature. Most manufacturer targets are hot pressures. A typical track tire gains 3-6 psi from cold to hot depending on ambient temperature, pace, and compound.

If you only have cold pressures to work with, keep a log of the cold-to-hot delta at a given track in similar conditions. That delta is your fastest tuning signal.

## What pressure does

- Lower pressure softens the carcass, enlarges the contact patch, and builds more heat. Too low causes vague feel, overheating, and edge wear.
- Higher pressure stiffens the carcass, shrinks the contact patch, and sheds heat. Too high causes skating, a greasy feel, and center wear.

## Safe increments

Change pressure in 0.5 psi steps on motorcycles and 0.5-1.0 psi steps on cars. Change one axle at a time when diagnosing a balance issue. Re-measure hot pressures for at least one session after a change before drawing conclusions.

## Common symptoms

- Front pushing mid-corner after a pressure increase: you likely raised the front beyond its optimal operating window. Try dropping 0.5 psi.
- Rear spinning on exit with low hot pressure: tire is overheating; add 0.5-1.0 psi or let it cool between sessions.
- Both ends feel vague after a cold morning: pressures are below target once hot; raise cold pressures 1-2 psi.
