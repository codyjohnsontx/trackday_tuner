# Wet and mixed conditions

Wet conditions shift the limits of grip, tire operating windows, and the relevance of aero. Setup changes are usually small; driving style changes are usually large.

## Tires

- If running dedicated wet tires: follow the manufacturer cold pressure; do not copy a dry baseline.
- If running dry tires in damp conditions: lower cold pressures 1-2 psi to enlarge the contact patch and help the tire warm. Be aware of aquaplaning risk at speed.
- Avoid drastic pressure changes session-to-session if the track is drying; the window will move under you.

## Suspension

Soften compression slightly (1-2 clicks) to help the tires conform to the wet surface and find mechanical grip. Keep rebound close to dry baseline; too soft becomes floaty under throttle.

## Aero (cars)

Rear downforce still matters in the wet and often helps more than in the dry because mechanical grip is reduced. Do not reduce wing angle for a wet session unless you need a specific straightline advantage.

## Driving and riding

- Early, smooth throttle application beats late, aggressive inputs.
- Brake earlier and in a straight line; trail braking is unforgiving in the wet.
- Find the dry line first (it is usually not the dry racing line). Curbs and sealer strips stay slick longer than they look.

## Safety

Wet sessions are where small errors become large. Informational setup advice cannot substitute for your judgment about whether conditions are safe for your experience level.
