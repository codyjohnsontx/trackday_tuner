title: Rear Spin on Exit
tags: [rear spin, traction, exit]
---

Rear spin on exit can come from many places, but the first checks should stay simple and mechanical. Start with rear tire condition and hot pressure. If the tire is overworked, overheated, or outside its normal pressure window, chasing clickers before confirming the tire is usually wasted effort.

After tire basics, look at rear ride height, sag, and throttle pickup context. If the rear is riding too high or not settling into the corner exit phase, drive grip may drop. If the bike squats excessively, it can also overload the tire and make grip inconsistent. The point is not that every spin issue is suspension, but that ride attitude and contact patch load matter.

Review what changed before the symptom appeared. A gearing change, a pressure change, a rebound adjustment, or a different tire condition may explain the new problem more directly than general theory. In many trackside cases, the session log is the fastest clue.

If the rider says the rear feels busy and starts spinning as throttle is picked up, a small rebound or support change may be worth checking only after tire and geometry basics look sensible. Avoid stacking multiple changes at once, especially if the tire itself might be the limiting factor.

Most importantly, note whether the spin happens at first pickup, at full lean, or later as the bike is stood up. Those are different problems and should not be treated as the same generic exit issue.
